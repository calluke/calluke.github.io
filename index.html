Cronulla-Sutherland Sharks

History

The Cronulla-Sutherland Sharks are an Australian professional rugby leauge team based in cronulla, in the sutherland shire.The sharks, as they are commonly know, were admitted to the New South Wales Rugby Leauge premiership and joined the National Rugby Leauge competition, in january 1967.The club competed in every premiership season since then. The sharks have been in the competition for 49 years, appearing in four grand finals, winning thier first premiership in 2016 after defeating the melbourne storm at ANZ stadium.

2016 Finals

After the 2016 season in whichthe sharks finished third, they went on to defeat the raiders in the Week 1 qualifying final 16-14 and earn the week off. Advaning to the preliminary final, they defeated defening premiers North Queensland cowboys 32-20 at Allianz Stadium. They would face and defeat the Melbourne Storm in the 2016 NRL Grand Final. The Sharks, after leading 8-0 at half-time, prevailed in a gripping affair 14-12 to claim their first premiership in the club's 50-year history. Sharks second rower Luke Lewis was awarded the Clive Churchill Medal as Man of the Match.
